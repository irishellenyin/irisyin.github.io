I am currently a second-year graduate student at Nanjing University, majoring in astrophysics. I started working on CubeSat development and gamma-ray burst (GRB) data analysis during my sophomore year and earned my B.S. in Physics from Nanjing University in 2023. I plan to pursue a Ph.D. and an academic career in the coming years.

My primary research interest is the GRB central engine and its associated astrophysical phenomena. The focus of my recent researches has been on analyzing and interpreting the prompt emission phase of GRB observations, and understanding different astrophysical phenomena related to the central engine, especially of the peculiar ones, such as GRB 211211A, GRB 230307A and GRB 231115A.

Additionally, as an STP-3 and STP-6 member of Einstein Probe (EP) Mission, I concentrate on the multi-messenger observations and follow-ups of EP-detected GRBs. I have led the data analysis of the first EP-detected GRB 240219A, and serve as the Transient Advocate (TA) in EP observations.
